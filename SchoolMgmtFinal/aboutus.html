About Us

Welcome To Shree Amar Singh Secondary School

Shree Amar Singh Model Secondary School is one of the most sought-after schools of the country. It was established by the Central Co-ordination Board of Government of India by utilizing the “Post War (First and Second World Wars) Reconstruction Fund” on 13 Chaitra 2013 B.S . The school now is a centre for the students of secondary and higher secondary education.

At present, the school is running +2 science stream in the day shift, and +2 Humanities, Management and Education streams in the morning. Thousands of plus two students have been graduated from this institution, and among them some are either pursuing further education in Medicine, Engineering, and Chartered Accountancy, etc., or have been placed in responsible positions making significant contribution in the society.

The science block of amarsingh contains 4 sections for each semester out of them 1 section is for computer, 2 for biology and 1 mixed section along with well equipped laboratories(physics, chemistry, zoology, biology, computer). Library service is also available for the students as long as they have their identity card.

Parts of School:



LABORATORY: The laboratory is the very essence of a school. At Amar Singh, in addition to other branches, the science branch also has four laboratories: Physics, Chemistry, Zoology, and Botany. These laboratories are spacious, well-equipped, technology-enabled, and well-organized. With the assistance of experienced lab technicians who have been working for years, students are allowed to use the laboratory during their free time, outside the routine schedule, if they show interest in gaining additional knowledge. In the laboratories, students are divided into cooperative groups, and the teachers' explanatory demonstrations are highly effective.



COMPUTER LABORATORY: In line with the demands of modern times, we have separate computer labs for every level in every stream. There is also a dedicated arrangement for the computer group in the science stream. With highly branded and advanced modern technology-equipped computers, students can practice on them at any time as per their course requirements. For classes 11 and 12, the computer lab is used for four sessions, with one group for pure and one group for mixed classes in each session.



LIBRARY: At the center of the school, accessible to students of all streams and levels, lies a large central library. Students can borrow books from the library according to their level, including those prescribed by the NEB, recommended by teachers, or commonly used in the market, and take them home for study. They can also spend their free time studying in the library itself.



EXAMINATION: In alignment with the academic calendar published by the Metropolitan Office and NEB, a planned calendar is published for each level, including information about exams, holidays, and extracurricular activities. Separate calendars are also published for each level. According to the calendar, the exam details are as follows:

a. Monthly Exams: 3-4 times
b. Trimester Exams: 2 times
c. Send-Up or Qualification Exam: 1 time
d. Annual Exam: 1 time
e. Re-Examination: 1 time
f. MCQ Tests: 2-3 times

Through interaction with parents about exam results, we identify students' problems, seek solutions accordingly, provide counseling, and conduct regular training and evaluation. Based on the results, additional classes and individualized guidance are offered as part of remedial efforts.



EXTRACURRICULAR ACTIVITIES: The school premises include a well-equipped sports ground. To ensure students' mental and physical development, excellent arrangements for sports activities are in place. The school also provides a conscious environment to nurture and showcase the inherent talents of students, such as leadership, artistic skills, humor, calligraphy, quizzes, olympiads, dance, public speaking, and acting. Opportunities for leadership or socialization are provided by allowing students to represent their class or the school. Free calligraphy practice sessions are also arranged for students in grade 12 to help them achieve excellent marks in board examinations.



CAFETERIA: There are three cafeterias available within the school premises: one at the eastern gate, one at the northern gate, and one in the science section. They operate from 6 AM to 4 PM, providing both snacks and meals to students at affordable and convenient prices.

Scholarships:


1. Full Scholarship for the Top Scorer: Students securing the highest marks (one male and one female) in the entrance exam conducted for admission to Grade 11 (after the publication of SEE results) will receive a full scholarship covering admission and monthly fees.

2. 50% Scholarship for Top Ten: Students ranking in the Top Ten (below the first position) in the entrance exam for Grade 11 will be awarded a 50% scholarship on admission and monthly fees.

3. Terminal Examination Scholarship: After enrollment, the school will grant a scholarship equivalent to a 50% discount on three months' monthly fees to the top 3 male and top 3 female students who excel in the internal terminal exams.

4. Other:

a. Financial resources received from generous donors were used to provide immediate assistance to some students in various categories based on their needs at different opportunities.
b. After the class 12 board exams, 10-12 students were recommended for scholarships with various facilities based on their needs and abilities for preparatory classes provided by institutes such as VIBRANT, PEA, NAME, etc.




Enrollment Process:


The enrollment process for Grade 11 is outlined below:
1. The distribution of forms for enrollment will open as soon as the SEE results are published.
2. The office will be open from 6 AM to 11 AM for Management, Education, and Humanities streams, and from 10 AM to 4 PM for Science stream.
3. The form, which is available online, can be downloaded, printed, and submitted at the office along with the necessary documents.
4. When submitting the form, an interview will also be conducted, so the concerned student must be present.
5. All students who meet the qualifications as per the instructions from the Board or the Ministry of Education will be eligible to fill out the form.
6. The result will be published the day after the exam, and will be available by Roll Number. The result will be posted on the school’s social media (Facebook) and on the gate. Additionally, a message will be sent to the mobile numbers of successful students. Therefore, please provide the official contact number when filling out the form. After receiving the notification and passing the result, students must appear with the admission card and their guardians. Since seating will be based on a first-come, first-served basis, make sure to secure your seat immediately. Students arriving late will not have their seats reserved.



Dress Code:


At Shree Amarsingh Secondary School, both boys and girls are required to wear the same type of dress. Girls are not allowed to wear skirts. Everyone must wear government-issued blue pants, a blue coat, a white shirt, black shoes, and black belt (if worn), along with blue socks. The tie and badge will be provided by the school at the time of enrollment. In winter, students may wear sweaters, gloves, caps, scarves, jackets, or hoodies, but these must be in a matching blue color with the pants. Additionally, students in the Science labs must wear a sky-blue apron.



Conduct:


1. Students must be present on time every day. If absent, the school must be notified. If a half break is needed during class hours, parents must come to pick up the student.
2. Mobile phones are not allowed at school. If it is necessary to bring one, parents must inform the school. Even if the mobile is brought with prior notification, it must be handed over to the office, not taken to class. If found in class, the school will take appropriate disciplinary action.
3. Wearing gold or silver jewelry for display is not allowed at school.
4. Dyeing hair or maintaining it in an inappropriate manner is not allowed. Hair must be kept neatly at all times.
5. Students are not allowed to wear nails painted or with nail polish.
6. Applying henna or any other form of adornment on hands is not permitted.
7. Except for traditional earrings, no fashion earrings or accessories are allowed in the ears.
8. In case of a dispute, the matter must be brought to the administration for a judicial decision.
